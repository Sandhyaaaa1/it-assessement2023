Leo Club of Kathmandu Launches Successful Tree Plantation Campaign on 1st February

Date: February 1, 2024

Location: Kathmandu, Nepal

In a bid to combat environmental degradation and promote sustainable living, the Leo Club of Kathmandu launched a robust tree plantation campaign on the 1st of February. The initiative, aimed at addressing the pressing issue of deforestation, saw enthusiastic participation from club members and volunteers alike.

Under the clear skies of Kathmandu, the members of Leo Club assembled at designated sites across the city, armed with saplings and shovels. The campaign kicked off early in the morning with a brief ceremony, where club officials reiterated the importance of preserving and nurturing the environment for future generations. With unwavering dedication and a shared sense of responsibility towards the environment, the volunteers diligently planted a diverse range of tree species native to Nepal. From the iconic rhododendrons to the sturdy sal trees, each sapling symbolized a step towards a greener and more sustainable future. The campaign did not merely focus on planting trees but also emphasized the need for their proper care and maintenance. Volunteers received hands-on training on watering techniques and soil management to ensure the survival and growth of the newly planted saplings. Speaking on the occasion, Ms. Alisha Thapa, the President of Leo Club Kathmandu, expressed her gratitude towards all the participants and emphasized the club's commitment to environmental conservation. She remarked, "Today marks the beginning of our journey towards a greener and healthier Kathmandu. Through collective action and community involvement, we can make a tangible difference in combating climate change and preserving our natural heritage." The tree plantation campaign garnered widespread attention and support from local residents and authorities alike. Representatives from the Kathmandu Metropolitan City praised the efforts of the Leo Club and pledged their continued support towards similar initiatives in the future. As the sun began to set on the horizon, the volunteers concluded the day's activities with a renewed sense of purpose and accomplishment. The sight of rows of freshly planted saplings served as a poignant reminder of the power of collective action in safeguarding the environment. The success of the tree plantation campaign by the Leo Club of Kathmandu stands as a testament to the enduring spirit of community service and environmental stewardship. With initiatives like these, Kathmandu takes significant strides towards a greener and more sustainable future.
